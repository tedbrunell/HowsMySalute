How Is Your Salute?

This is a simple computer vision program to try to determine if someone's "salute" meets U.S. Army standards. Army TC3-21.5 covers the requirements for a proper salute.

From Army TC 3-21.5:

    	Raise the right hand sharply, fingers and thumb extended and 
	joined, palm facing down, and place the tip of the right 
	forefinger on the rim of the visor slightly to the right of the 
	right eye. The outer edge of the hand is barely canted downward 
	so that neither the back of the hand nor the palm is clearly 
	visible from the front.  The hand and wrist are straight, the 
	elbow inclined slightly forward, and the upper arm horizontal

This script attempts to track your joints and body parts to determine if you meet the requirement:

  • Upper Arm is parallel to the ground
  • Forearm is at a 45 degree angle
  • Wrist and hand are in-line with the forearm
  • Palm is not visible
